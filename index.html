Literature Survey

Collaborative learning is a type of all-around learning in which teachers assign learning tasks, control the teaching process, and permit students to collaborate. Online science education may unquestionably assist students in obtaining a solid grasp of scientific concepts. Literacy in computing and other forms of information technology is also essential. In the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, students were asked to sign up for e-learning courses. This was done so that they could participate in online classes.

The study found that the lack of internet access or WIFI made students feel unmotivated and disengaged. Affective dimensions appeared to suffer as a result of the E-learning system's failure to facilitate actual social and psychological interactions between instructors and pupils. Collaborative e-learning is contemplated as a pedagogical proposal owing to the fact that students mutually help each other to acquire knowledge. Having the tutor/instructor as the process facilitator and technology is viewed as a great allied in the socio-cognitive development of the students. Qualities such as engagement skills, and leadership skills, thus respecting to others.

Research Gap

The benefits of the collaborative learning can be categorized under four main categories which can be describe as social, psychological, academic, and assessment benefits. Learners are always given the sensation of virtual classmates to experience the typical classroom atmosphere. The system administrator can set the number of classmates, which also has the effect of turning this feature on or off altogether. Teachers can use a web app to predefine the questions that a child must solve. This gives the quiz enough flexibility to allow them to repeat it in case of failure. Researchers developed a system that allows students to improve their collaborative learning skills while playing a game.

Research Problem

Students can improve their communication skills, leadership skills, interpersonal skills, and a wide variety of other abilities by participating in collaborative learning activities. At some point, kids will not be able to learn on their own. Students should be prepared to participate actively and productively in a knowledge-based society. The administration of quizzes to students is highly recommended for fostering collaborative learning. Students are encouraged to discuss their perspectives while working together to complete the task. If there is a way to provide quizzes with a time limit and giving the answers from student's voice and from a digitally created worksheet, it would be a huge opportunity for students to share their knowledge.

Objectives

Main Objectives

Design and develop a collaborative learning platform to encourage collaboration with interactive, visually rich experiences that build foundational skills and enhance learning through working together.

Sub Objectives

  • Design and develop a component where the teacher can create a collaborative activity with required guidelines and the system will auto generate the answer. This component will use Natural Language mechanisms to do content verification. And submitted answers will be auto assess by the component and give feedback to the group.
  • Design and develop a component to improve to how to do an experiment in a virtual laboratory, how the inter communication occurs between the students, how to do activities collaboratively in a virtual laboratory by using Machine Learning and related technologies.
  • Design and develop a component to improve the subject-based knowledge, develop communications skills and team-work skills by using a group-based gamification activity based on voice recognition and Image Processing.
  • Design and develop a component to improve the skills to draw subject-based diagrams with having a customizing toolbox to improve the creativity of this collaborative activity by providing different questions by using data analyzing, figure identification and image processing.
